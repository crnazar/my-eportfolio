Christina Restrepo Nazar, Ph.D.

crn@ucf.edu  |  407.369.2772  |  christinarestreponazar.com

Dr. Christina Restrepo Nazar is a researcher whose scholarship sits at the intersection of science education, digital humanities, and AI studies, animated by the ways in which love, devotion, and humanistic understanding shape the conditions for genuine learning and transformation. Drawing on her formation as a science educator, her Catholic roots, and her current doctoral work in the Texts & Technology Ph.D. at the University of Central Florida (UCF), she approaches educational questions as fundamentally philosophical: What does it mean to know? How do human beings come to care about ideas? And how might technologies either open or foreclose the contemplative dimensions of learning?


Her research spans four interconnected areas: the philosophical foundations of teacher preparation; STEM learning environments that cultivate genuine intellectual devotion; digital and multimedia learning tools as sites of philosophical inquiry; and the ethical and cultural implications of artificial intelligence in lifelong learning. Her scholarship is guided by the conviction that rigorous philosophical inquiry and contemplative ways of knowing together constitute the most honest response to the humanistic challenges of our technological moment.
